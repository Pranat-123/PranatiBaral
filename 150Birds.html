150birds

A marketing startup that arranges a team of freelancers to curate a marketing team for clients.

BUSINESS CHALLENGE

The 150birds CEO has requested a website re-design to more effectively present company offerings.

SOLUTION

A website and graphics re-design resulting in an increase in engagements and clientele.

ROLE

Marketing Iterations to the Website

Design System Overview

Before making the changes to the website, I studied certain aspects of the design system that are important to consider when doing the website redesign.

First, I studied the color palette to determine how I should incorporate the brand colors into the website

After, I looked at the website to see which typography 150birds uses.

Home Page

On the home page, I added a section to highlight reviews from previous clients by adding a quote from senior client stakeholders as well as their ratings. In addition, I also created a carousel to highlight previous successful projects.

Customer Reviews

As my goal was to emphasize 150birds' positive impact with its clients to build credibility with future clients, I added a light yellow background to contrast the black text and made this section as big as the screen.

Case Studies

Similar to the customer reviews, I wanted to draw attention to the case studies so I added a bright yellow backdrop. I also placed elements about the designated on a light yellow/beige backdrop to increase the contrast. To add some interactivity in the home page, I put the case study cards into an automatic carousel.
Framework Page

In addition to the home page, I created a an infographic for the framework page to demonstrate the client journey and how the 150birds process works.

Pricing Page

To showcase the company offerings, I created a pricing page demonstrating the services and subscriptions that 150birds offers.

Takeaways and Next Steps

Through designing the website, I learned about common UI elements that are prevalent in every website. For instance, successful brands commonly use blue for their call-to-action buttons as it symbolizes trust.

Due to tight deadlines, it was difficult to incorporate user testing and research to design the website. If I had more time and resources like a user experience designer, I would coordinate with them to research and create wireframes as well as prototypes prior to making the final version.
